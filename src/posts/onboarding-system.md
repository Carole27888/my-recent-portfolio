*By Carole Mutemi | Virtual Assistant & Social Media Manager*

When most people hear "Nairobi," Instagram management for a New York wedding photography studio is not the first thing that comes to mind. But that is exactly what I do, and it works better than you might think.

I have been managing the social media presence for a wedding photography studio based in New York for over a year now. The photographer shoots stunning, documentary-style weddings. My job is to make sure the world sees his work consistently, strategically, and in a way that turns profile visitors into booking enquiries.

Here is how it actually works.

---

## The Setup

Before I touched a single post, I spent the first two weeks learning the brand. I went through every photo in the gallery, watched how the photographer shot, what moments he gravitated towards, what made his work different from the thousands of other wedding photographers on Instagram.

You cannot manage someone's social media if you do not understand what makes them worth following.

I use a combination of Airtable for content planning, Later for scheduling, and a shared Google Drive folder where the photographer drops raw selects after each shoot. We have a weekly check-in on Monday mornings — fifteen minutes, sometimes less — where we align on what is coming up and anything he wants to push.

Everything else I handle independently.

---

## What I Actually Do Each Week

The week-to-week work breaks down into three things:

**Content selection and editing.** From a gallery of 600 to 800 images from a single wedding, I select 8 to 12 that tell the story of that day. I look for the hook image — the one that stops the scroll — and I build the sequence around it. The editing is light. The photographer's work does not need heavy filters. It needs the right crop and the right order.

**Captions and hashtags.** Every caption is written to feel personal, not promotional. Wedding photography is an emotional purchase. People are not buying photos — they are buying the feeling that someone will capture their most important day properly. The captions reflect that. The hashtags are a three-tier strategy mixing broad reach, mid-size, and hyper-niche tags.

**Reels strategy and scheduling.** Reels are where the reach happens. We post three to four Reels per week, each between twenty-five and thirty seconds, structured around a hook in the first three seconds. One Reel alone generated over 157,000 views, reached 83,000 unique accounts, and drove 46 new followers in a single week.

---

## What the Timezone Difference Actually Means

Nairobi is seven hours ahead of New York. In practice, this means I am working while the photographer is asleep, and his content is scheduled and ready before his morning coffee.

It also means I am never competing with his day. He shoots. I handle the rest.

The only adjustment we made early on was making sure I understood his posting windows — Tuesday and Thursday evenings New York time, Saturday mornings. Those are his peak engagement windows. I schedule everything to land in those slots regardless of what time it is on my end.

---

## What This Has Done for the Business

Since taking over the account, monthly reach has grown by over 1,000 percent. Profile visits have increased from under 300 per month to over 4,800. Enquiries now come in through Instagram DMs on a weekly basis.

More importantly, the photographer spends zero time on his phone worrying about posting. He shoots. I handle the rest.
